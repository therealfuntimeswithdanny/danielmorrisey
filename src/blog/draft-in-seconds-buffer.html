Today Buffer made a huge update to the app, so naturally the first thing I did was open the Shortcuts app.

Yeah, that’s right. Now I’m on the official Discord, and I remember Andy from Buffer and another Discord
user talking about composing posts via shortcuts, so today I gave it a shot.

I opened the Shortcuts app and started dragging and dropping like a mad man.
Then this is what ended up coming out.

A simple shortcut that does this:

  • Get Current Date & Time
  • Start dictating text (speech-to-text)
  • Copy dictated text to clipboard
  • Save a new idea to Buffer
    • Org = Your Organization
    • Title = Current Date & Time
    • Content = Copied text in clipboard (dictated text)
  • Wait 1 second (to let the Buffer app do its thing)
  • Say "Idea Saved"
  • Show text "Post saved to ideas"

We can do Better

Right now, this shortcut works, but if you look closely… you see that “…was imposed via…”
that’s meant to be "Composed” but we can fix that very easily if your iPhone has Apple Intelligence.

Unlike mine, so that makes things a little bit more tricky.

Using chatGPT to help

Before we begin, you do need the ChatGPT app on your iPhone and a signed-in account.

This does add extra steps and can easily use up all your free daily credits for GPT-5.

So after making some edits, here’s what we have now.

A not so simple short cut that does this:

  • Get Curent Date & Time
  • Dictate text (speech-to-text)
  • Copy Dictated Text to Clipboard
  • Open ChatGPT (app)
    • Make a new chat
    • Send Prompt: "Can you Proofread this as a Bluesky Post,
      only show the edited text: {paste text from clipboard}"
    • Wait for a Responce
    • Copy Responce to Clipboard
  • Create new idea in Buffer
    • Org = Your Organization
    • Title = Current Date & Time
    • Content = Copied text in clipboard (chatGPT Responce)
  • Wait 1 second (to let the Buffer app do its thing)
  • Say "Idea Saved"
  • Show text "Post saved to ideas"
